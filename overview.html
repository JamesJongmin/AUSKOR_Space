Australia Roadmap Australia
2018-2030
Australia's Strategic Space Development Path
Australia's space industry has transformed from $8.0B market (2023) targeting $12B by 2030. Key milestones: Australian Space Agency established (2018), $3B total investment 2018-2023 ($1.1B private sector since 2019), Space Infrastructure Fund ($19.5M), 814 space organizations by FY2023. Strategic priorities: Position Navigation & Timing (PNT), Earth Observation, Space Situational Awareness, Communications, Leapfrog R&D, Robotics & Automation. Target: 30,000 space jobs by 2030 (current: 19,595). Focus areas: launch infrastructure (Arnhem Space Centre expanding to 300 hectares), satellite manufacturing, space domain awareness (EOS tracking systems), and lunar robotics through Moon to Mars initiatives.

Strategic Partnership Collaboration
2021-2035
Bilateral Cooperation Framework Evolution
Comprehensive Strategic Partnership established December 2021 with space cooperation as key pillar. December 2021 MoU between Australian Space Agency and Korea's Ministry of Science and ICT enables collaboration in Earth observation, robotics, space manufacturing, and launch. Regular Space Policy Dialogue established 2022 for coordination. Active partnerships: Hanwha-Gilmour Space MoU (September 2024) for joint launches, INNOSPACE-Equatorial Launch Australia multi-year contract for Arnhem Space Centre launches starting 2025. Priority collaboration areas: small satellite development (<100kg), permanent Korean launch site in Northern Australia, joint Space Situational Awareness, KSLV-III component integration, 2032 lunar lander collaboration, Earth Observation data sharing, next-gen satellite communications.

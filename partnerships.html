Australia-Korea Strategic Space Partnership

Strategic Space Partnership
Comprehensive Strategic Partnership Framework (Established December 2021)
üî• ACTIVE Collaboration
September 2024
Hanwha-Gilmour Space Major MoU Signed
Hanwha Defence Australia, Hanwha Aerospace, and Gilmour Space Technologies signed major MoU at Land Forces 2024. Leveraging Hanwha's defense expertise with Gilmour's Eris rocket and Queensland orbital launch facilities. Immediate goals: joint R&D, co-development of integrated launch technologies, and collaborative launches of Korean payloads from Australian territory. Addressing regulatory, legal, and export challenges to position as Indo-Pacific space services leaders.
üî• ACTIVE Collaboration
2024-2025
Innospace-Equatorial Launch Australia Partnership
Korean firm INNOSPACE signed multi-year contract with Equatorial Launch Australia for orbital launches from Arnhem Space Centre in Northern Territory. Inaugural launches scheduled for 2025. Australia's equatorial location provides significant launch cost advantages and safety benefits by avoiding populated areas.
FRAMEWORK Collaboration
December 2021
Bilateral MoU on Cyber & Critical Technology
Australian Space Agency and Korea's Ministry of Science and ICT MoU forms part of Comprehensive Strategic Partnership. Enables collaboration in Earth observation, robotics and automation, space manufacturing, and launch. Regular Space Policy Dialogue established in 2022 for coordination.
Small Satellites Collaboration
Joint Small Satellite Development (<100kg)
Priority collaboration area for surveillance and reconnaissance satellites. Korea's 3-year small satellite lifespan creates need for international partnership. Australia's Earth Observation from Space Roadmap emphasizes international collaboration to strengthen manufacturing capabilities. Joint development builds domestic infrastructure and skills in both nations. Surveillance targeting North Korea's nuclear capabilities provides mutual security benefits.
Launch Infrastructure Collaboration
Permanent Korean Launch Site in Australia
Proposed dedicated Korean launch site in Northern Australia leveraging equatorial proximity for lower launch costs. Australia's Arnhem Space Centre expanding to 300 hectares. Streamlined regulatory arrangements for launch permits, export licenses, and cross-border rocket movement. Korean investment would benefit local Australian economies while providing Seoul strategic launch capabilities distant from potential threats.
Space Situational Awareness Collaboration
Joint SSA & Space Traffic Management
Increased collaboration in space security as operational warfighting domain. Integration of EOS Mt Stromlo and Learmonth tracking systems with Korean SSA networks. HEO's Non-Earth Imaging capabilities complement Korean space surveillance. Building resilient Indo-Pacific space domain awareness architecture against counterspace operations and cyber threats.
Launch Vehicle Technology Collaboration
KSLV-III Component Integration Opportunities
Australian technologies for KSLV-III reusable launch vehicle: Conflux Technology's 3D-printed heat exchangers, Advanced Navigation's precision guidance systems, MOOG's satellite control systems, and Neumann Space's electric propulsion. Bilateral technology transfer and co-development reducing costs and accelerating development timelines.
Lunar Exploration Collaboration
2032 Lunar Lander Collaboration
Australia's Roo-ver technology and remote asset management expertise for Korea's 2032 lunar lander mission. Australian strengths in robotics, autonomous systems, and harsh environment operations (mining sector) complement Korean spacecraft capabilities. Both nations participating in NASA Artemis program enables trilateral cooperation framework.
Earth Observation Collaboration
Data Sharing & AI Analytics Platform
Integration of Digital Earth Australia with Korean KOMPSAT and reconnaissance satellite data. Joint AI-powered analytics for agriculture, climate change, disaster management, and defense applications. Could revive Australia's cancelled National Space Mission for Earth Observation at lower cost through collaboration. LatConnect 60 AI technology partnership opportunities.
Communications Collaboration
Next-Gen Satellite Communications
Korean telecommunications leadership with Australian satellite communications infrastructure. EM Solutions' King Cobra terminals integration with Korean LEO constellations. Joint development of direct-to-mobile 5G/6G services, optical communications, and quantum secure communications. SmartSat CRC high-speed communication terminal technology collaboration.
Critical Technologies Collaboration
AUKUS Pillar 2 Cooperation Potential
Australia, US, and UK expressed interest in exploring Korea joining AUKUS Pillar 2 for advanced capability cooperation. Would facilitate smoother export controls and strategic goods flow for satellite development and launch collaboration. Korea ranks highly in satellite-related research per ASPI Critical Technology Tracker. Joint leadership in defense-relevant technologies: AI, quantum computing, advanced materials.
Investment Collaboration
Cross-Border Venture Capital & Startups
Link Australia's Space Infrastructure Fund ($19.5M) with Korea's government-private matching space fund (doubled by 2027). Support bilateral startup market entry and technology partnerships. Joint investment in emerging space technologies. Creating Indo-Pacific space innovation ecosystem.
Supply Chain Collaboration
Critical Minerals & Space Manufacturing
Australia's critical minerals capabilities (lithium, rare earths, nickel) with Korea's advanced manufacturing capacity. Space-qualified component manufacturing leveraging Korean expertise in electronics and materials science. Resilient supply chains countering Chinese economic coercion that both nations have experienced.
Workforce Collaboration
Talent Exchange & Training Programs
Bilateral internship and researcher exchange programs between Australian Space Agency and KASA. STEM education collaboration leveraging 60+ years of scientific partnership. Joint space mission simulation and training. People-to-people links pillar of Comprehensive Strategic Partnership creating sustained cooperation framework.
Regional Leadership Collaboration
Indo-Pacific Middle Powers Alliance
Both nations as "middle powers" demonstrating flexible, agile alliance can shape regional security and technological innovation. Template for Indo-Pacific allies developing dual-use capabilities, enhanced deterrence, and economic growth via space cooperation. Building regional resilience through control of critical infrastructure, supply chains, and space-based data. Partnership positioned as proactive response to growing counterspace threat landscape.
